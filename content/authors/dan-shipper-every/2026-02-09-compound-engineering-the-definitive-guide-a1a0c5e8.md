<p>Software used to be built by armies of engineers. Now, with AI, Every runs all five of its products with single-person engineering teams.  </p>
<p>When I started building <strong><u>Cora</u></strong>, our AI email assistant, from scratch, I wanted to see how much I could enable myself with AI. I knew it was possible for one person to <u>ship like five</u>. All I had to do was build the right systems and the right productivity hacks.</p>
<p>This evolved into a systematic approach to AI-assisted development that I call compound engineering. It now has 7,000 stars on GitHub, which confirms my belief that this will become the default for how software gets built. </p>
