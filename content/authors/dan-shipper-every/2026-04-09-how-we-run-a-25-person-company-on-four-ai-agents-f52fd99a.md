At any given moment, each person has roughly 30 tasks on their to-do list. So how do they figure out which to work on first? </p><p>The team used to rely on <strong><u><a href="https://every.to/@brandon_5263" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Brandon Gell</a></u></strong>, Every’s COO, to run traffic control and coordinate the whole company, which required him to manually cross-reference launch calendars, company strategy documents, and task lists. Each one automates a different task that, without the agent, would require tedious logistical work to track and schedule. Each one draws on the same set of interconnected databases that the team already maintains. </p><p>At our first <u><a href="https://every.to/events" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Custom Agents Camp</a></u>, produced in partnership with <u><a href="https://www.notion.com/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Notion</a></u>, Brandon and Every head of growth <strong><u><a href="https://every.to/@tedescau" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Austin Tedesco</a></u></strong>, walked more than 500 subscribers through four agents they’ve built, the databases underneath them, and how to create your own. Notion product designer <strong>Brian Levin</strong> also joined to share best practices from the Notion team.</p><h4><strong>Key takeaways</strong></h4><ol><li><span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span><strong>Describe the outcome, not the steps.</strong> Tell the AI what you want to accomplish and let it figure out the implementation. Over-prescribing (“Create a database, then add a relation, then filter by...”) tends to confuse the model. </li><li><span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span><strong>Your Notion is your agent’s brain.</strong> Custom agents get powerful when they can query interconnected databases. Every’s agents work because strategy, calendar, tasks, people, and meeting notes all live in Notion and reference each other.</li><li><span class="ql-ui" contenteditable="false"></span><strong>Don’t write the agent’s instructions yourself.</strong> Tell Notion AI what you want the agent to accomplish, and it will generate the instructions.
